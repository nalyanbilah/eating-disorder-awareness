Understanding

What Are Eating Disorders?

Eating disorders are serious mental health conditions characterized by persistent disturbances in eating behaviors, thoughts, and emotions. They affect people of all ages, genders, and backgrounds.

Anorexia Nervosa

  • Extreme restriction of food intake
  • Intense fear of gaining weight
  • Distorted body image perception
  • Dangerously low body weight

Bulimia Nervosa

  • Recurrent binge eating episodes
  • Compensatory behaviors (purging)
  • Self-evaluation based on body shape
  • Feeling out of control during binges

Binge Eating Disorder

  • Eating large amounts rapidly
  • Eating when not physically hungry
  • Feelings of guilt and shame
  • No regular compensatory behaviors

Recognition

Signs & Symptoms

Early recognition can save lives. Look out for these warning signs in yourself or loved ones.

Physical Signs

Dramatic weight changes, fatigue, dizziness, feeling cold, hair loss, dental problems

Behavioral Signs

Skipping meals, excessive exercise, frequent bathroom visits after eating, rigid eating patterns

Emotional Signs

Anxiety around food, body image concerns, mood swings, depression, social isolation

Social Signs

Avoiding social meals, wearing baggy clothes, withdrawing from friends and activities

Recovery

Path to Balanced Eating

Healing your relationship with food is a journey. Here are some guiding principles.

Nutritional Balance

  • Include all food groups in meals
  • Eat at regular intervals
  • Honor your hunger and fullness
  • No foods are "good" or "bad"

Mindful Eating

  • Eat without distractions
  • Savor flavors and textures
  • Practice gratitude for food
  • Listen to your body's needs

Body Positivity

  • Appreciate what your body can do
  • Focus on health, not weight
  • Challenge negative thoughts
  • Celebrate non-physical qualities
