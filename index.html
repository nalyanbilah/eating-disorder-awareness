Types of Eating Disorders

Understanding different types of eating disorders helps in recognizing symptoms and seeking appropriate help.

Anorexia Nervosa

Characterized by weight loss, difficulties maintaining appropriate body weight, and distorted body image.

Learn more →

Bulimia Nervosa

A cycle of binge eating followed by behaviors to prevent weight gain, such as self-induced vomiting.

Learn more →

Binge Eating Disorder

Recurrent episodes of eating large quantities of food, often quickly and to the point of discomfort.

Learn more →

OSFED

Other Specified Feeding or Eating Disorder includes atypical presentations of eating disorders.

Understanding the Causes

Eating disorders are complex conditions that arise from a combination of biological, psychological, and social factors.

Brain

Neurological factors including genetics, brain chemistry, and neurotransmitter imbalances can contribute to eating disorders.

Heart

Emotional factors such as low self-esteem, perfectionism, and difficulty expressing emotions play a significant role.

Digestive

Physical complications and digestive issues can both contribute to and result from disordered eating patterns.
